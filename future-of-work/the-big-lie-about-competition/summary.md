# The Big Lie About Competition

**Author:** Shaan Puri (@ShaanVP)
**Date:** March 13, 2026
**Source:** https://x.com/ShaanVP/status/2032568123556245834

## Key Takeaways

- Most people who say "it's too competitive" are using that as an ego-protecting lie to avoid taking action.
- Out of 1,000 people who want the same thing, the vast majority are "unserious" -- they self-eliminate through inaction, quitting early, moving too slowly, or chasing distractions.
- The largest group of "competitors" never even tries -- they are too afraid to start.
- The next biggest chunk gives up after just 3 failed attempts, long before any real persistence is tested.
- Others disqualify themselves by moving too slowly or getting distracted by shiny objects.
- Your actual competition is vanishingly small: maybe one other person who is serious. The real barrier is not the crowd -- it is your own seriousness and follow-through.
- Charlie Munger's principle applies: "take a simple idea, and take it seriously" -- seriousness of execution is the differentiator, not the idea itself.

## One-Line Summary

Competition is an illusion because the vast majority of people self-eliminate through fear, quitting early, slowness, or distraction -- leaving only a tiny handful of serious contenders.
