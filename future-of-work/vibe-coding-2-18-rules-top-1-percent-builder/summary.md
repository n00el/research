# Vibe Coding 2.0: 18 Rules to be the Top 1% Builder

**Author:** Harshil Tomar (@Hartdrawss)
**Date:** February 24, 2026
**Source:** https://x.com/Hartdrawss/status/2026198305362083910

## Key Takeaways

- Vibe coding 2.0 is not about typing less with AI -- it is about making the right architectural and tooling decisions before writing any code, because bad early decisions silently compound into velocity killers.
- Never build auth, realtime infrastructure, or deployment pipelines from scratch for an MVP; use managed services (Clerk, Supabase, Vercel) so you spend energy on what differentiates your product.
- Adopt a specific, opinionated stack early (shadcn/ui, Tailwind, Zustand, tRPC, Prisma, Zod) to eliminate decision fatigue and reduce integration overhead.
- Set up error tracking (Sentry) and analytics (PostHog/Plausible) from day one -- flying blind in production and making product decisions on assumptions are the two most common regrets.
- Use version control with feature branches even as a solo developer, because AI agents make confident mistakes at speed and you need a reliable way to recover.
- Schedule regular technical debt cleanup after every 2-3 features to prevent the codebase from becoming unmaintainable, and document architectural decisions so future contributors understand the tradeoffs.
- Ship imperfect MVPs fast and iterate; the goal is learning, not polish -- perfectionism is the real velocity killer.

## One-Line Summary

The best vibe coders do not write better code -- they ruthlessly identify what not to build, choosing proven tools and managed services so all their energy goes toward shipping and learning.
