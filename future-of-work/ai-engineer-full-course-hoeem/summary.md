## Key Takeaways

- AI engineering has 3 layers: prompt engineering (foundation) → context engineering (multiplier) → intent engineering (strategic)
- Prompt engineering is the least important of the three; context and intent engineering matter more
- 7-component intent framework: objective, outcomes, health metrics, strategic context, constraints, delegation protocol, stop rules
- Klarna cautionary tale: AI saved $60M but alienated customers by optimizing speed over experience
- MCP described as "USB-C port for AI applications" for standardized tool/data access
- Intent engineering is the antidote to "vibe coding" — strict contracts replace informal descriptions

## One-Line Summary

A three-layer AI engineering framework arguing that context engineering and intent engineering (with a 7-component schema) matter far more than prompt engineering alone.
