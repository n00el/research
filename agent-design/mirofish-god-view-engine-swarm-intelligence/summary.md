# MiroFish: The God View Engine

**Author:** BuBBliK (@k1rallik)
**Date:** March 14, 2026
**Source:** https://x.com/k1rallik/status/2032837113247395992

## Key Takeaways

- MiroFish is an open-source AI prediction engine that spawns thousands of autonomous agents with unique personalities, memories, and behavioral logic into a simulated world, then observes their interactions to generate prediction reports.
- Built in ~10 days via "vibe coding" with Claude Code by a Chinese undergraduate (Guo Hangjiang), it topped GitHub's Global Trending list in March 2026 with 25,000+ stars.
- The system uses a five-step pipeline: knowledge graph construction (GraphRAG), agent creation with persistent memory (Zep Cloud), dual-platform parallel simulation (OASIS engine supporting up to 1M agents), report generation, and interactive analysis.
- Shanda Group founder Chen Tianqiao committed 30 million yuan in funding within 24 hours of seeing the demo, valuing the developer's problem-solving approach over raw technical execution.
- Applications span financial forecasting, policy impact testing, public opinion simulation, crisis modeling, and even creative exploration (predicting novel endings from character behavior).
- Key limitation: no published validation benchmarks against real outcomes -- the tool is designed for scenario exploration rather than precise forecasting, and LLM agents may exhibit greater herd behavior than real humans.
- The predecessor project BettaFish also reached #1 on GitHub Trending in late 2024, establishing a pattern of building viral AI tools with rapid iteration.

## One-Line Summary

MiroFish is an open-source swarm intelligence engine that drops thousands of personality-driven AI agents into a simulated world to predict real-world outcomes, built in 10 days by a Chinese undergrad and backed by $4M+ in funding.
