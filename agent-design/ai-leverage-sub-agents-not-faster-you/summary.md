# You're Using AI as a Faster Version of Yourself. That's Not Leverage.

**Author:** George from prodmgmt.world (@nurijanian)
**Date:** Mar 13, 2026
**Source:** https://x.com/nurijanian/status/2032407066409943259

## Key Takeaways

- Naval Ravikant's leverage framework (labor, capital, code) needs updating: AI agents combine labor's output capacity with code's permissionless accessibility, creating a new hybrid form of leverage.
- AI agents lack the coordination failures that make labor leverage "messy" -- no conflicting career goals, no performance management, no ego about repetitive work.
- Most PMs use AI sequentially (one chat, one prompt, wait, repeat), which is just a faster version of yourself, not actual leverage.
- The E-Myth parallel applies: skilled technicians doing all the work themselves instead of building systems that do the work for them.
- Sub-agents enable parallel execution: spin up multiple agents simultaneously (customer research, spec drafting, backlog cross-referencing) and your bottleneck shifts to synthesis and judgment, which is the right bottleneck.
- The real constraint is your mental model -- whether you treat AI as a faster tool or as a team you direct -- and this matters more than any prompting technique.

## One-Line Summary

AI agents represent a new form of leverage that combines labor's output with code's permissionless access, but only if you use them as parallel sub-agents rather than a single sequential assistant.
