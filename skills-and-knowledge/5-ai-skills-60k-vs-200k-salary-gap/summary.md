## One-Line Summary

Five applied AI skills -- context engineering, workflow design, output QC, augmented analysis, and systematic integration -- are creating a $140K salary gap between AI-aware and AI-skilled professionals heading into 2027.

## Key Takeaways

- **Context engineering beats prompt engineering**: Building the right environment (background info, constraints, tone, business logic) so AI produces expert output without perfect prompting every time is now a $150K-$200K skill.
- **AI workflow design is not automation**: It is the ability to map where AI can and cannot replace human judgment in a process, then chain multiple AI steps into reliable outputs at scale -- turning 4-person workflows into 1-person operations.
- **AI output quality control is critical**: Domain experts who can systematically catch, diagnose, and fix AI errors are more valuable than the AI tools themselves, enabling lean AI-assisted teams.
- **AI-augmented analysis does not require coding**: Directing AI to find patterns and produce actionable insights from data is making non-technical professionals operate at analyst/strategist level.
- **Systematic AI integration is the rarest skill**: Most enterprise AI rollouts fail due to poor integration design; professionals who can make AI stick inside real workflows and organizations are being pulled into leadership roles.
- **The window is narrowing**: These skills are currently learnable without technical backgrounds, but competition for high-income AI roles will increase significantly in the next 6-12 months.
- **Awareness is no longer a differentiator**: Simply using AI daily became table stakes ~18 months ago; what matters now is applied, structured capability.
