# If you don't understand AI by the end of this, the next decade will confuse you
**Author:** Anish Moonka (@AnishA_Moonka)
**Date:** March 15, 2026
**Source:** https://x.com/AnishA_Moonka/status/2033128021092073502

## Key Takeaways
- The AI industry is a five-layer stack (Energy, Chips, Cloud, Models, Applications) where most profit currently concentrates in the infrastructure layers (1-3), not the application layer (5) that consumers see
- Hyperscalers (Amazon, Microsoft, Google, Meta) are spending $650-700B in combined capex in 2026, with ~75% ($450B) going directly to AI infrastructure -- buildings, chips, cables, cooling
- NVIDIA ($215.9B FY2026 revenue, 92% GPU market share), TSMC (70% foundry market), and ASML (sole EUV supplier) represent extreme concentration at the chip layer, creating both an investment thesis and geopolitical risk
- AI model companies (OpenAI, Anthropic) are growing revenue at historic rates but burning cash even faster -- OpenAI projects $17B cash burn in 2026 with positive cash flow not expected until 2029-2030
- The pattern mirrors historical infrastructure buildouts (electricity 1880-1920, internet fiber): early wealth creation happens in picks-and-shovels, applications capture value later
- Key risks include capital misallocation ($650B+ data centre spend may outpace AI service revenue), supply chain concentration (TSMC in Taiwan), and efficiency breakthroughs like DeepSeek that challenge the "more compute = better AI" assumption
- Investment strategy: infrastructure layers now (energy, chips, cloud), application layer later; the companies that will win at the app layer are ones sitting on proprietary data moats

## One-Line Summary
AI value creation currently flows to infrastructure (energy, chips, cloud) not applications, following the same "picks and shovels" pattern as every prior computing platform shift, with extreme concentration in NVIDIA, TSMC, and ASML creating both opportunity and systemic risk.
