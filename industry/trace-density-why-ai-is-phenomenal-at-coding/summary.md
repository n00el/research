# Trace Density: Why AI Is Phenomenal at Coding

**Author:** Animesh Koratana (@akoratana)
**Date:** Thu Mar 12 2026
**Source:** https://x.com/akoratana/status/2032119242276188424

## Key Takeaways

- AI excels at coding not because of precise syntax or training volume, but because of "trace density" -- the ratio of recorded reasoning to recorded outcomes in software's historical artifacts (commits, PRs, issues, discussions).
- Open source culture forced every contributor to justify decisions regardless of seniority, creating an unprecedented archive of documented reasoning that other professions (law, medicine, finance) never built.
- Software has uniquely immediate, unbiased feedback loops (compilers, test suites, production failures) that force reasoning to be recorded while it's still fresh, unlike human-mediated feedback in other domains.
- 30 years of version control, code review, and open source produced the richest reasoning archive in human history -- and AI trained on it.
- AI agents can deliberately generate trace density for other professions by recording their reasoning as they execute tasks, not just their outputs.
- How you build agents matters as much as whether you build them: agents engineered to capture decision traces create compounding value that outcome-only agents miss.

## One-Line Summary

AI is best at coding because software uniquely preserved 30 years of decision-making reasoning (not just outcomes), and agents can now deliberately build this "trace density" for every other profession.
