# A Framework for What Comes Next

**Author:** Radigan Carter (@radigancarter)
**Date:** Mar 20, 2026
**Source:** https://x.com/radigancarter/status/2035073252134129757

## Key Takeaways

- The Iran war will cascade through markets in 4 phases: Denial (now), 6-week energy price trigger (mid-April), summer earnings damage + AI-accelerated job losses (July-Aug), and Fed capitulation with rate cuts (late 2026-2027)
- The 6-week mark is the critical threshold -- after that, energy price increases are irreversibly embedded in freight, food, and consumer goods regardless of ceasefire
- Qatar's Ras Laffan LNG facility damage (20% of global LNG trade) could take years to repair, creating cascading supply chain effects from fertilizer to copper mining
- AI adoption will accelerate as a survival mechanism during the downturn -- companies facing margin compression from $95 oil will replace workers with AI tools, compounding cyclical unemployment
- The buying opportunity comes in Phase 3 (summer 2026) when quality tech and AI-adopting companies hit meaningful discounts before the Fed cuts rates
- Energy independence will become the dominant bipartisan political narrative in 2026-2027, benefiting U.S.-jurisdiction assets and energy infrastructure plays
- Iran has escalation dominance over both energy exports and desalination infrastructure, affecting 64 million people in the Gulf region

## One-Line Summary

A 4-phase framework for how the Iran war creates a stagflationary shock that compresses tech valuations through summer 2026, with AI-accelerated job losses forcing a Fed rate cut by September and creating generational buying opportunities.
