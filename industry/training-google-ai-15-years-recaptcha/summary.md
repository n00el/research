# You've been training Google's AI for 15 years. You had no idea.

**Author:** Sharbel (@sharbel)
**Date:** Mar 17, 2026
**Source:** https://x.com/sharbel/status/2033921312716882384

## Key Takeaways

- reCAPTCHA was invented by Luis von Ahn in 2007 to dual-purpose spam prevention as OCR for digitizing 130 million books from the New York Times archive and Google Books
- After Google acquired reCAPTCHA in 2009, they pivoted it from text OCR to image labeling of Google Street View data -- training computer vision models for Google Maps and Waymo
- At peak, 200 million reCAPTCHAs were solved daily, representing 500,000 hours of free human labor per day -- equivalent to $5 million+ in annotation costs daily at market rates
- The labeled dataset was unreplicable by any annotation company: billions of images across every city, weather condition, and time of day, generated as mandatory entry cost across the entire internet
- reCAPTCHA v3 (2018) eliminated visible challenges entirely, instead harvesting behavioral data (mouse movement, scrolling, hover patterns) to feed back into Google's AI systems
- Waymo, now valued at $45 billion with 4 million paid rides in 2024, built critical training data foundations on this unpaid human annotation

## One-Line Summary

The history of how Google's reCAPTCHA evolved from a book digitization project into the most successful invisible data annotation operation ever, generating billions of labeled images that built Google Maps and the $45 billion Waymo self-driving car company -- all from unpaid internet users.
