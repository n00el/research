# 10 Years Building Vertical Software: My Perspective on the Selloff

**Author:** Nicolas Bustamante (@nicbstme)
**Date:** February 16, 2026
**Source:** https://x.com/nicbstme/status/2023501562480644501

## Key Takeaways

- LLMs collapse proprietary learned interfaces into a single chat paradigm, destroying the switching-cost moat that justified $15K-$25K per-seat pricing at companies like Bloomberg and FactSet.
- Complex business logic that previously required years of domain-expert engineering can now be encoded as markdown skill files by non-engineers in weeks, vaporizing the workflow moat.
- Vertical software faces a pincer attack: hundreds of AI-native startups entering from below (10 engineers vs. 200), while horizontal platforms like Microsoft Copilot and Anthropic Claude extend into vertical territory from above.
- The three durable moats are proprietary/irreplaceable data, regulatory and compliance lock-in, and transaction embedding -- companies with none of these are high risk regardless of current revenue.
- The stock selloff reflects multiple compression rather than immediate revenue decline; enterprise contracts lock in 12-24 months of revenue, but the market is correctly repricing the end of premium multiples.
- Competition in each vertical will explode from 3 incumbents to 300 entrants, because the barrier to building an 80%-capable product has dropped by an order of magnitude.
- AI agents are quietly building their own system of record by aggregating context across all tools a user touches, threatening even the "canonical source of truth" moat long-term.

## One-Line Summary

LLMs are destroying the five moats (learned interfaces, encoded workflows, data accessibility, talent scarcity, bundling) that kept vertical software defensible, while leaving regulatory, network, transaction, and proprietary-data moats intact -- forcing a permanent repricing of the entire sector.
