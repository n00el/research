# The Vibe Coding Trap

**Author:** Max Musing (@MaxMusing)
**Date:** February 12, 2026
**Source:** https://x.com/MaxMusing/status/2021990855741936072

## Key Takeaways

- The cost of building software is collapsing, but the cost of owning software (maintenance, compliance, ops) remains high and is often overlooked.
- Software ownership is a liability, not an asset: you inherit every future bug, regulatory change, and operational burden the moment you build it yourself.
- AI-driven productivity gains make undifferentiated internal work more expensive, not less, because each engineering hour now has higher opportunity cost.
- SaaS products survive by absorbing operational surface area — you pay for the service, not the code.
- The SaaS products that will thrive are those where the gap between MVP and production-grade is enormous (e.g., Stripe, Cloudflare), because they are hard to own, not hard to build.
- "Mediocre SaaS" that offers little beyond code is genuinely threatened; high-operational-burden SaaS is not.

## One-Line Summary

AI makes building software nearly free but makes owning it relatively more expensive, which means SaaS products that absorb operational complexity will become more valuable, not less.
