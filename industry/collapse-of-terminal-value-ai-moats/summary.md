# The Collapse Of Terminal Value - What Happens If AI Makes Every Moat Temporary?

**Author:** Chamath Palihapitiya (@chamath)
**Date:** March 16, 2026
**Source:** https://x.com/chamath/status/2033385903520129161

## Key Takeaways

- If AI makes competitive moats temporary, equities should be repriced from current 22-60x multiples down to 2-7x FCF, reflecting only near-term cash flows rather than long-duration terminal value.
- A repricing to 5x FCF would collapse the S&P 500 from ~$58T to ~$14T -- a 75% drawdown dwarfing the 2008 financial crisis.
- Historical precedent exists: newspapers, retail, energy, and taxi medallions all saw similar multiple compression when markets recognized finite cash flow duration.
- Growth investing and venture capital would effectively cease to function, as there is no terminal value to grow into when moats are temporary.
- Capital would rotate to physical, AI-insulated assets: energy infrastructure, farmland, toll roads, water rights, commodities, and short-duration sovereign bonds.
- The scenario is self-defeating: if markets reprice, AI capex ($300-500B/yr) becomes unfinanceable, disruption slows, moats become durable again -- creating an oscillating regime rather than a permanent shift.
- Sovereign wealth funds and state capital (US, China, Gulf states, Norway, Singapore) would gain structural advantage as the only entities capable of long-horizon investment.

## One-Line Summary

Chamath argues that if AI erodes competitive moats fast enough to make cash flows unpredictable beyond five years, the resulting collapse in equity terminal values would trigger a 75% market drawdown -- though the scenario is likely self-correcting as depleted AI investment would slow the very disruption causing the repricing.
