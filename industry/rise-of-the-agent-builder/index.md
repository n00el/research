# The Rise of the Agent Builder

**Author:** Zach Lloyd (@zachlloydtweets) — Founder & CEO of Warp
**Date:** March 4, 2026
**Source:** https://x.com/zachlloydtweets/status/2029223875066687683
**Stats:** 75 likes, 7 retweets, 1 reply

---

Zach Lloyd argues that companies need a dedicated full-time role -- the "Agent Builder" -- whose job is to systematically replace SaaS tools and manual workflows with AI agents. He draws on Warp's own internal experience building agents on their Oz cloud agent platform.

## Core Thesis

For companies to pull ahead, they need to systematically replace SaaS tools and manual workflows with agents, and dedicate a full-time role to do exactly that.

## Warp's Agent Builders

Their first Agent Builders are Emily (automating Growth team work) and Dave (building agents for product intelligence). Their mandate is to use Warp's cloud agent platform Oz to automate tasks.

## Key Points

1. **Coding agents generalize well:** They were initially uncertain since Oz was built around coding agents, but discovered that a great coding agent makes a great general-purpose agent.

2. **Replacing SaaS subscriptions:** These agents complete tasks that Warp might have purchased SaaS tools for a few months ago -- such as social listening and user feedback analysis. They are actively cancelling SaaS subscriptions in favor of custom-built agents.

3. **Concrete example — Fraud detection:** An agent that constantly looks for suspicious usage patterns and creates PRs to block users, while also identifying novel fraud patterns.

4. **Cultural shift:** The old internal mandate at Warp was "start with a prompt" to get help with a task. The new mandate is to evaluate whether the task can be automated on an ongoing basis by building an agent.

5. **Challenges:** Building internal agents involves up-front environment setup, data access grants, monitoring, and prompt tuning. Prompt tuning is best served by dogfooding early with the team, tolerating initial rough edges, and turning feedback into prompt improvements.

6. **Agents vs. structured workflows:** It has never been easier to automate a tedious task without a structured, Zapier-like workflow. An agent, given the right capabilities and skills, can use intelligence to decide on the right workflow instantly -- no need to predefine "if this, then that" steps.

## Prediction

> "Within a year, every company over 50 people will have at least one person whose full-time job is building internal agents."
