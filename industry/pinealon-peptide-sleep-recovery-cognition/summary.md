# Summary: Pinealon Peptide - Sleep, Recovery & Cognition

## One-Line Summary

Pinealon is a Russian-developed tripeptide (EDR) that claims to dramatically improve sleep architecture, reduce brain fog, and enhance cognitive function by working at the epigenetic level rather than through receptor binding.

## Key Takeaways

- **Pinealon is a tripeptide bioregulator** (glu-asp-arg) developed by Professor Vladimir Khavinson in Russia starting in the 1980s, originally for the Soviet military and space program to protect cosmonauts' brains under extreme stress.
- **Mechanism is epigenetic, not receptor-based:** Unlike most nootropics/drugs, pinealon crosses cell and nuclear membranes to interact directly with DNA, modulating gene expression of protective proteins (SOD2, GPX1) and reducing apoptosis enzymes (caspase-3).
- **Sleep efficiency over sleep duration:** Users report compressing 8 hours of recovery into 6 hours, with increased REM and deep sleep phases. The author personally reports waking before alarms with zero brain fog after 2 weeks of 200 mcg daily subcutaneous injections.
- **Works both orally and via injection:** Unlike most peptides, pinealon's small tripeptide structure survives digestion and crosses the gut barrier intact, making oral supplementation viable.
- **Clinical evidence exists but is predominantly Russian:** Studies show benefits for TBI recovery (72-patient trial), working memory improvement (59.4% of subjects), EEG alpha-index increases, and neuroprotection in Alzheimer's and Huntington's disease models.
- **Cycling protocol recommended:** 30 days on, 1-4 months off. Effects persist weeks to months after cessation due to epigenetic modifications. Over-use may paradoxically worsen sleep.
- **Not a stimulant but a recalibration agent:** Works best for people with compromised baselines (brain fog, poor sleep, chronic stress) rather than pushing already-optimized systems beyond normal function.
