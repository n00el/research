# You've Been Training Google's AI for 15 Years

**Author:** Sharbel (@sharbel)
**Date:** 2026-03-17
**Source:** https://x.com/sharbel/status/2033921312716882384

## Key Takeaways

- reCAPTCHA extracted 500,000 hours of free human labor daily at peak (200M solves/day at 10 sec each), worth ~$5M/day in annotation costs
- Google pivoted reCAPTCHA from book digitization (OCR) to Street View image labeling in 2012, directly feeding training data into Google Maps and Waymo
- Waymo's $45B valuation and 4M+ paid rides are built partly on ground truth data annotated by unwitting internet users solving CAPTCHAs
- reCAPTCHA v3 (2018) silently tracks mouse movement, scroll behavior, and hover patterns -- no visible challenge, no opt-in, continuous behavioral fingerprinting
- No annotation company could replicate Google's approach because they made labeling mandatory as the price of accessing any website, creating a global-scale data factory

## One-Line Summary

Google turned a ubiquitous security mechanism into the world's largest unpaid data annotation pipeline, building tens of billions of dollars in products (Maps, Waymo) on human labor users never consented to provide.
