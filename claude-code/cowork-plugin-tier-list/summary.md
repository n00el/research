# I Tested All 21 Claude Cowork Plugins. Here's the Tier List Nobody's Publishing.

**Author:** Nav Toor (@heynavtoor)
**Date:** Feb 26, 2026
**Source:** https://x.com/heynavtoor/status/2027054100807106829

## Key Takeaways

- Data Analysis, Productivity, and Sales are the three S-tier plugins worth installing immediately; Data Analysis alone found a $14K/month pricing anomaly a client's data team missed for two quarters.
- Plugins are just markdown files (skills, slash commands, connectors, sub-agents), making the barrier to building custom plugins essentially zero — 2,000 GitHub stars and private enterprise marketplaces already exist.
- The Legal plugin is credited with triggering $285B in SaaS stock losses ("SaaSpocalypse"), but lands at A-tier because it requires heavy org-specific customization to be production-ready.
- Wave 1 (Jan 30) targeted horizontal functions (productivity, sales, data); Wave 2 (Feb 24) shifted to vertical/industry-specific plugins (IB, PE, equity research, HR, engineering), signaling Anthropic's platform ambitions.
- Most B-tier and below plugins ship with generic defaults — their real value unlocks only after substantial company-specific customization of templates, tone, escalation criteria, and workflows.
- The compounding effect matters: plugins paired with good context files and global instructions over time turn Claude from a smart generalist into something closer to a domain-expert chief of staff.
- A $20/month subscription is now doing roughly 40% of what $150/month enterprise SaaS seats do, which is why markets repriced incumbents so violently.

## One-Line Summary

The top three Cowork plugins (Data Analysis, Productivity, Sales) deliver immediate, measurable ROI out of the box, while most others require significant customization — but because plugins are just markdown, the real disruption is that building domain-specific AI workflows now has near-zero cost.
